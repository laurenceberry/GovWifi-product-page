Get started

Check GOV.UK Verify is right for your service

GOV.UK Verify could be right for your service if it needs to:

  • have legal confirmation that a user is who they say they are, for example if they’re signing a mortgage deed
  • show a user’s personal information to them, like their driving licence or passport details
  • pay money to a user
  • give the user something valuable, like a Blue Badge or a council house

You might not need GOV.UK Verify if you only need to recognise a returning user. For example, if you’re making an appeal to the tax tribunal you can save your appeal and return to it later by creating an account.

To help you decide if GOV.UK Verify is right for your service, you should think about:

  • what your service lets users do
  • whether you need to know the identity of your users, or whether knowing they are the same person every time will be enough
  • whether you need to be able to take users to court if they’ve been fraudulent
  • whether any other controls are in place to prevent fraud

Organisations that can use GOV.UK Verify include:

  • central government
  • local government
  • NHS
  • police
